<p><p>Calcium Silicate Insulation is categorized into two primary market types based on temperature resistance: High Temperature Calcium Silicate Insulation and Mid Temperature Calcium Silicate Insulation. High Temperature Calcium Silicate Insulation is designed to withstand extreme heat applications, often exceeding 1,200°F (650°C), making it suitable for use in industrial settings like refineries and power plants. Mid Temperature Calcium Silicate Insulation, on the other hand, is tailored for moderate temperature environments, typically ranging from 1,000°F (538°C) to 1,200°F (650°C), and is commonly used in HVAC systems and equipment insulation.</p></p>
