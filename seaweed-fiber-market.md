<p><ul><li>Staple</li><li>Filament</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The seaweed fiber market is primarily classified into two types: staple fibers and filament fibers. Staple fibers are shorter, processed from seaweeds like kelp, and are commonly utilized in textiles, composites, and non-woven fabrics. They are valued for their biodegradable properties and versatility. Filament fibers, on the other hand, are long, continuous strands derived from specific seaweed species, primarily used in high-performance textiles and specialty applications. Both types cater to the growing demand for sustainable and eco-friendly materials in various industries.</p></p>
