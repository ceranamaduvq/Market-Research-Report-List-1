<p><p>The spinal non-fusion device market is divided into two primary types: metal and polymer non-fusion devices. Metal non-fusion devices are typically made from materials like titanium or stainless steel, providing strength and durability in spinal stabilization without fusing vertebrae. In contrast, polymer non-fusion devices utilize materials such as PEEK (polyether ether ketone) that are lighter and can facilitate greater flexibility and biocompatibility. Both types aim to relieve pain and improve mobility while maintaining spinal motion, offering alternatives to traditional fusion surgery.</p></p>
